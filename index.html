Mosaic Labs

Building interfaces for AI-assisted collaboration and deliberation

Mosaic is an experimental research lab focused on reducing the risks from advanced AI by improving the quality of human reasoning. We build interfaces for AI-mediated collective reasoning and deliberation, and conduct research into how AI assistance can help humans collaboratively solve problems.

Rapid developments in AI are quickly transforming the future of reasoning, communication, and conflict. Our goal is to ambitiously apply AI to collective deliberation problems, explore the frontier of human-machine collaboration, and to keep human reasoning at the center of institutional decision-making.

Differential Development

When atomic energy was developed, people gained the power both to produce green energy and to kill millions. They did not gain the wisdom to use that power responsibly, nor the ability to ensure their power was used cooperatively for the good of all.

To safely navigate the risk of advanced AI, we need technology which enables coordination, empowers human groups to think clearly, and prevents disastrous conflicts. The order in which technology is developed matters. We aim to accelerate work which uses recent advances in AI capabilities to enable better collective reasoning and coordination, and gives people the ability to navigate an uncertain future.

Architectures for Cooperation

Our mission is to improve human reasoning at the group level, focusing on the ways in which people communicate information and resolve disagreements. In particular, we are prototyping the use of AI to mediate group deliberation. This means both using AI to intervene on the dynamics of a discussion, as well as being an interface for people to better understand the content of what is being discussed. We are pioneering new architectures for group dialogue and reasoning, finding ways to integrate the unique abilities of AI systems while keeping humans and human reasoning at the center.

Code as a Catalyst

We are building experimental interfaces which test new strategies for AI-mediated collective deliberation. By testing and demonstrating new methods, we aim to grow a movement directed toward improved human reasoning, large scale collaboration, and coordinated action.

Software can often serve as a nucleation site for dialogue, and become a part of the ontology we use to think about the future (e.g. federated social networks, Polis/Community Notes, or Loom). Our philosophy is to advance the discussion about AI development by building software which embodies our vision for the future.

Scaling to Superhuman AI

AI systems are improving rapidly. For this reason, it's important that work to augment human reasoning improves alongside AI capability advancements, and is not replaced or made obsolete by them. Better human reasoning and decision making is critical to navigating the transition to superhuman AI safely, and making the right choices about how to engineer the future we want.

We are especially concerned that important reasoning may be totally outsourced to AI systems. For this reason we are dedicated to keeping humans at the center of our interfaces, and focus our research on how AI mediation can support entirely human-lead reasoning and discussion.
