Mosaic Labs

Building interfaces for collective intelligence and AI-mediated deliberation

About

We develop tools that help groups think better together. Our work explores how AI can enhance collective reasoning while keeping humans meaningfully in the loop.

As we approach transformative AI capabilities, the quality of our collective decision-making becomes increasingly critical. We believe that by thoughtfully integrating AI into collaborative interfaces, we can augment human deliberation rather than replace it. Our tools help groups explore disagreements, find unexpected consensus, and make better decisions together.

Current projects include experimental interfaces for structured group deliberation, disagreement mapping, and collaborative sense-making. Through these practical explorations, we're working to ensure that human agency and collective wisdom remain central as AI capabilities advance.

We're actively researching and prototyping new ways for AI to support group reasoning - from helping surface common ground in contentious discussions to identifying productive directions for further exploration. Our goal is to develop interfaces that grow more capable alongside AI while remaining deeply human-centered.

Differential Development

When atomic energy was developed, people gained the power both to produce green energy and to destroy entire cities. They did not gain the wisdom to use that power responsibly, nor the ability to ensure their power was used cooperatively for the good of all.

To safely navigate the risk of advanced AI, we need technology which enables coordination, empowers human groups to think clearly, and prevents disastrous conflicts. The order in which technology is developed matters. We aim to accelerate work which uses recent advances in AI capabilities to enable better collective reasoning and coordination, and gives people the ability to navigate an uncertain future.

Cooperative Architectures

Our mission is to improve human reasoning at the group level, focusing on the ways in which people communicate information and resolve disagreements. In particular, we are prototyping the use of AI to mediate group deliberation. This means both using AI to intervene on the dynamics of a discussion, as well as being an interface for people to better understand the content of what is being discussed. We are pioneering new architectures for group dialogue and reasoning, finding ways to integrate the unique abilities of AI systems while keeping humans and human reasoning at the center.

Code as a Catalyst

We are building experimental interfaces which test new strategies for AI mediate collective deliberation. By testing and demonstrating new methods, we aim to help grow a movement directed toward improved human reasoning, large scale collaboration, and coordinated action.

Software can often serve as a nucleation site for dialogue, and become a part of the ontology we use to think about the future (e.g. federated social networks, Polis/Community Notes, or Loom). Our philosophy is to advance the discussion about AI development by building software which embodies our vision for the future.
